FAQ

Do you have questions about tattoos?

We have the answers!

1. HOW MUCH DOES A TATTOO COST?

How much does a red car cost? As you can see, the question cannot be answered that easily. Prices are always based on the individual motif, size, style and part of the body. The elaboration and the number of details also play a role. Therefore, we can only give binding prices after thorough consultation on site. If you don't have the opportunity to visit the studio in person, we can also try to answer all your questions by phone or email and give you comprehensive advice.

2. I WOULD LIKE TO GET ADVICE, DO I NEED AN APPOINTMENT FOR THIS?

No, you can simply visit our studio without obligation. However, it is best to call in advance to avoid long waiting times. Feel free to bring some ideas with you so that we can get a better picture of your ideas.

3. HOW DO I GET AN APPOINTMENT?

You are welcome to visit us personally in the studio for a consultation. We can discuss all your wishes on site and can then find the right date for you. In order to book your appointment bindingly, we need an appointment deposit. The amount of the deposit always depends on the cost of the tattoo and can vary. If you are not able to come by in person, you can make an appointment by phone and transfer the deposit. Please note that the appointment is only reserved for you once payment has been received.

4. WHY CAN'T I MAKE THE APPOINTMENT VIA FACEBOOK / INSTAGRAM?

In order to select the right artist for you and to discuss all the details, it is necessary for you to be there in person. Some factors in particular (such as the condition of the skin, any scar tissue that may be present, etc.) can only be assessed with binding effect on site. Pictures are usually not enough because, depending on the exposure, they do not reflect the actual condition of the skin. In addition, a deposit must be paid so that the appointment is firmly scheduled for you. Unfortunately, this is only possible on site. Discussing a tattoo request can often be very extensive. There are many factors that play a major role in a successful tattoo. Therefore, a personal conversation is always the best way to help you get your dream tattoo.

5. WHY DO I HAVE TO PAY A TERM DEPOSIT?

We schedule the tattoo artist for you. During this time he cannot attend any other customer appointments. We create an individual design for you according to the specifications that we received from you during the consultation. And so of course we want to make sure that you keep your appointment.

6. HOW LONG IS THE WAITING TIME FOR YOU?

The waiting time depends on the time required for the tattoo, the artist and the style. Therefore, waiting times can vary greatly. As a rule, however, we can offer appointments very promptly.

7. WHEN CAN I DISCUSS MY MOTIF WITH THE TATTOO ARTIST?

You can come to our studio at any time for advice. Our team will be happy to advise you comprehensively on your wishes and ideas. If necessary, we will of course involve the artist. Keep in mind, however, that you also wouldn't want your appointment constantly being interrupted so that your tattoo artist can talk to other customers. You have enough time at the appointment to discuss everything with each other.

8. WHEN CAN I SEE THE TEMPLATE?

The template will be created together with you at the appointment. A rough sketch is often made in advance, but you do the finer details together with the artist. It is therefore usually not possible to see or send the draft beforehand.

9. WHEN WILL MY TATTOO BE TOUCHED UP?

Once your tattoo has healed, you should stop by for a check-up. This is usually the case after 4-6 weeks. On site we can see exactly what and how much needs to be touched up and arrange a suitable appointment with you directly. Since there are more and more cancellations of follow-up appointments, we require a deposit of €20, which you will of course receive back on the follow-up appointment if you attend it as agreed.

10. I AM NOT YET OF LEGAL AGE. CAN I STILL GET A TATTOO?

We only tattoo from the age of 18! It is therefore necessary that you can present a valid identification document (identity card, passport, driver's license) to make an appointment and also to the appointment.

11. I AM PREGNANT / STILL BREASTFEEDING. WHY CAN'T I GET A TATTOO?

Apart from the fact that the immune system of a new mother is always on the alert to protect the child and infections can easily creep in (e.g. through fresh wounds), not only is a mother more at risk, but above all the child. The mother gives most of the antibodies to the child through milk to keep it healthy. So if a breastfeeding mother gets an infection, you will also find inflammation levels in the milk. This cannot be healthy for anyone. But it can get worse. During tattooing, we put foreign bodies under the skin. Since you bleed when you get stung, foreign bodies not only get under the skin, but also get into the blood. These are then transported away by the lymphatic system. You can think of it as a kind of customs office of the body, where certain foreign bodies are confiscated, so to speak. As long as you don't have any allergies and have a healthy immune system, that's not a problem. When you're still a baby, though, things are very different. 12. I TAKE MEDICATION. CAN I STILL GET A TATTOO?

You will not be tattooed with us if you are taking antibiotics, as your immune system is already weakened by the antibiotics and undesirable complications can occur when the tattoos heal. We recommend that you give your body at least 2 weeks of rest after you have fully taken the antibiotics before you get a tattoo. Blood-thinning medication is usually not an obstacle. However, these should be discontinued a few days before the appointment so that you do not bleed more and the color can be properly incorporated. However, you should always discuss this with your doctor in advance, as we cannot carry out any treatment in case of doubt. Caution should also be exercised with strong acne preparations, which must be discontinued at least two weeks before tattooing.

13. DOES TATTOOING HURT?

Yes - tattooing hurts. How painful it is for you depends on the body part, the length of the appointment and of course your own pain sensitivity.

14. WHAT ABOUT ALLERGIES?

Please inform us in advance about any allergies or intolerances. This is the only way we can react accordingly. This applies to contact allergies, eg latex, but also to ingredients in dyes or disinfectants. If possible, clarify with your doctor whether you can get a tattoo.

15. HOW CAN I CANCEL / RESCHEDULE MY APPOINTMENT?

Appointments can only be canceled/postponed by telephone or in person in one of our studios. This must be done at least 3 working days before your appointment so that we have enough time to find a replacement. If the appointment is not changed in accordance with the agreement or if you do not appear at the appointment, we will keep the appointment deposit. In the case of appointments that are changed in good time, the deposit remains or is of course paid out to the person who made the appointment upon presentation of the appointment card. If you become ill and rebook or cancel your appointment at short notice, please send us a copy of the medical certificate. If we are able to invite a replacement customer for your appointment at such short notice, your deposit will be retained. Otherwise, depending on the individual case, we decide whether we withhold the entire amount or only a partial amount for the loss of work that we have incurred. You are also welcome to give your appointment to a friend. To do this, you would have to contact us immediately and tell us what the person in question would like for a motif. If the desired motif can be implemented by the same artist, this should not be a problem. Cancellations via Facebook, SMS, email, WhatsApp etc. are not compliant and are considered void.
